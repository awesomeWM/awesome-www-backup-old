Details

The cursor often changes to waiting and never reverts (or timeouts). Trying to find a reason for this I disabled startup notification on every awful.util.spawn call in my rc.lua. But it kept happening - sometimes even out of the blue, without anything evident being "started".

I think I found one of the culprits, it will also happen when my IM client (Gajim) triggers one of it's own notifications pop-ups (event when unfocused).

Comment by Michael Lee (mlee) - Saturday, 09 May 2009, 00:30 GMT
I am also experiencing the persistent wait mouse cursor. Here is how I cause it:

1. Log in (awesome starts)
2. Start up konsole
3. Exit konsole

Work-around:
If after I've triggered the bug, I restart awesome (Meta-Ctrl-r), The cursor is restored and doesn't get stuck in the wait state again even if startup and exit konsole a second time.
Comment by anrxc (anrxc) - Saturday, 09 May 2009, 13:28 GMT
You could also reset the cursor with:
$ xsetroot -cursor_name left_ptr

If it's tedious to restart awesome for you (as we all know the clients are not put in their previous positions)... But that doesn't solve the point that awesome will still be waiting for "something" as the timeout never happens.
